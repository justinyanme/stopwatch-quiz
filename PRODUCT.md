# Product

## Register

product

## Users

This is for a single developer who uses CodexBar throughout the day and wants a physical, wrist-glanceable status surface instead of opening a Mac menu bar. The watch is used at a desk or on the wrist, often between coding tasks, so the interface must be readable in a second and usable with minimal button input.

## Product Purpose

CodexBar StopWatch mirrors CodexBar usage, spend, and balance data onto an M5Stack StopWatch over BLE. Success means the watch feels like a reliable instrument: current enough to trust, fast enough to glance at, and calm enough to leave running nearby without becoming distracting.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, instrumental.

The interface should feel like a compact status instrument rather than a phone app or dashboard. It can use vivid provider colors and polished motion, but the motion should clarify state changes and time, not compete with the data.

## Anti-references

Avoid smartwatch gimmicks, decorative animation, busy dashboard density, bouncy transitions, and marketing-style visual drama. Avoid any interaction that makes the device feel less dependable, including auto-advance behavior that fights manual navigation or interrupts detail inspection.

## Design Principles

1. Glance first: a user should understand the current view before the next auto-advance.
2. Motion explains navigation: transitions should reveal direction, continuity, and hierarchy without hiding data.
3. Manual control wins: button presses, touch scrolling, refresh, and sleep must override autoplay immediately.
4. Respect the physical display: animation must stay legible on a 466 px circular panel and avoid edge clutter.
5. Preserve trust: stale, loading, and link states remain visible and honest during animated changes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Reduced-motion behavior is required. Users who do not want continuous motion should be able to disable autoplay or use a non-animated transition mode. Color cannot be the only signal for status; existing labels and pills remain the primary status affordance.
